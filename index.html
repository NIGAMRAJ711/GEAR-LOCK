v3 mechanism — step by step
STEP 01
You pick a future date
Choose the exact UTC date you want the lock to be openable. This date is baked into the lock — not the hour or minute, just the day.
STEP 02
Gears spin at 1ms intervals
Each gear rotates once per millisecond at its own prime ratio (2×, 3×, 5×, 7×...). The hole on each gear aligns toward the center at a specific millisecond. That millisecond is the unlock_ts_ms — midnight UTC of your chosen date.
STEP 03
Password = alignment at unlock_ts_ms
The character at each gear's aligned hole at exactly unlock_ts_ms forms your password. Stored once, never re-shown.
STEP 04
On unlock day — ±500ms window
On the unlock date, the system checks if your password matches any millisecond within ±500ms of your submission time. This handles network latency without widening the attack surface.
STEP 05
Midnight passes — self-destruct fires
At 00:00 UTC the day after unlock, the reaper thread finds the lock, overwrites all sensitive fields with random bytes, deletes from all data structures, and zeros memory. Nothing remains — not the config, not the PIN hash, not the lock ID.
STEP 06
Even with server access — nothing to find
After self-destruct, a full server dump reveals no trace of the lock. The password was never stored. The PIN was never stored. The master secret was never stored. Everything derived on-the-fly, then erased.
why 1ms rotation stops brute force
→ Password changes every 1 millisecond = 86,400,000 different passwords per day
→ Minimum network round-trip = ~20ms = password changes 20 times before attempt arrives
→ Even if attacker guesses correctly, by arrival it's already wrong
→ ±500ms window exists ONLY for the legitimate user's stored password from creation time
→ Without the stored password + PIN, attacker must guess from ~86 billion daily combinations
→ Rate limit: 5 attempts per 5 min = max 1,440 attempts per day
→ 86,400,000,000 ÷ 1,440 = attacker needs ~60 million years to enumerate
